# Why this lab exists

So... why does one person have a pile of open-source repos about poking around inside language models?

I'll say it directly.

My goal is **not** to discover the next big mechanistic interpretability breakthrough.

My goal is **not** to get a new job.

My goal is **not** to raise funding.

My goal is actually very straightforward.

I want anyone building an AI application to be able to open their model during
inference, look inside, inspect what is happening, and experiment with the
newest interpretability ideas on their own use case.

Why?

For two reasons.

First, models are fascinating. I refuse to accept that "it's a black box" is
where the conversation ends. If science had accepted that something was "just a
black box," we'd never have kept looking deeper into nature.

Second, even though I left academia years ago, I never stopped believing in open
science. Some of the most exciting ML research still takes a long time to reach
people building real products. I've lived in both worlds, and I'd love to help
make that gap a little smaller.

The nice thing is that you don't need a particle accelerator to look inside a
language model.

A single GPU is enough for surprisingly interesting experiments.

Heck... I even started on a CPU. 🙂

If this resonates with you, let's connect.

Or just take a repo, open a model, and start asking questions.
